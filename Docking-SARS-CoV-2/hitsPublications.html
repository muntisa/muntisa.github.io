Different publications are proposing these 28 compounds (drugs or natural products) as possible solution for the COVID-19 treatment. Some of them are even tested into clinical trials. This section is presenting our docking interactions with 18 protein targets (virus or human). Please remember that this web tool is always running new targets or compounds.

Objective: Presenting the docking interactions for the best molecules in the current publications to propose active molecules against COVID-19.

Results: Resveratrol, vitamin D3, cucurmin and NMN shows better interactions that chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine on ACE2 receptor or SARS-CoV-2 main protease (Mpro). For the moment, the best interactions are obtained with Clofazimine, Apilimod and Nafamostat. Clofazimine is using for leprosy and interacts with DNA (at least in bacteria). More interesting is Apilimod, an inhibitor interleukins IL-12 and IL-23, and developed for the treatment of autoimmune conditions such as Crohn's disease and rheumatoid arthritis. Recently apilimod has been repurposed as a potential antiviral and anti-cancer drug, with possible applications in the treatment of non-Hodgkin lymphoma as well as viral diseases such as Ebola virus disease, Lassa fever and COVID-19. Thus, we confirmed the news from Sanford Burnham Prebys Medical Discovery Institute, the University of Hong Kong, Scripps Research, UC San Diego School of Medicine, the Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai and UCLA (see details from April 18, 2020).
